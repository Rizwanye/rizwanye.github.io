## Who I am

I am Rizwan Ye. I have worked across the Australian Taxation Office and EY,
with experience that sits between business analysis, software delivery, and
data-informed problem solving.

My academic background is a Bachelor of Computer Science with a major in
Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics. My honours work focused on
Explainable Artificial Intelligence, which sharpened my interest in how models,
evidence, and operational decisions connect in real systems.

This site is personal, but it has a clear theme: how useful technical work gets
from an idea into something people can actually use. That means understanding
the business context, asking better questions of the data, building maintainable
software, and learning how production systems are deployed and supported.

I publish the site under iRizwan as a personal working notebook, not a company
brand. The material here reflects my own learning path, technical judgment, and
interest in business-aware software that can survive production conditions.

## Why this site exists

I use iRizwan as an open-source record of a future role I care about: forward
deployment engineering. The site documents how to think about the role, how to
build toward it, and how I am personally working toward becoming one.

Some of the writing will be educational. Some of it will be practical notes
from learning, building, and working through technical problems. The common
thread is the same: useful work should not stop at a document, dashboard,
notebook, or prototype if it can become a reliable system.

## What I write about

The site focuses on a few connected topics:

- business analysis and requirements
- data science and practical forecasting
- software engineering for maintainable delivery
- cloud deployment with AWS
- open-source learning and public notes
- forward deployment engineering

## Forward deployment engineering

Forward deployment engineering is one way to describe the mix of skills I am
writing about. It is the label I use for work that starts with a business
problem, uses data honestly, and ends with software that can run in production.
The [forward deployment engineering](fde/index.md) section is where I break
that idea down in more detail, including the deeper [job of a forward
deployment engineer](fde/job/index.md).

That is why the topic matters here. It gives a name to the kind of work I want
to understand and explain, but it does not replace the personal side of the
site.

## What it means to me

I want the site to feel like a public notebook for learning, not a generic
marketing page. The personal layer matters because colleagues, friends, or
future collaborators should be able to understand both my background and the
direction I am building toward.

## What I am building toward

The direction of this site is to become a personal record of learning, building,
and explaining how business-aware software gets made. It is also a public record
of the path toward becoming a forward deployment engineer. The topics I expect
to write about here include:

- business analysis and requirements
- data science and practical forecasting
- software engineering for maintainable delivery
- cloud deployment with AWS
- open-source learning and public notes
- the forward deployment engineer skill set
