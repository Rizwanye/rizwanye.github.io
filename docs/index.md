## What a forward deployment engineer does

<div class="card-grid">
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Business lens</p>
    <h3>Start with the problem.</h3>
    <p>
      A forward deployment engineer does not begin with tools. The first step is
      understanding the business problem, the stakeholder, and the decision that
      needs to be made.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Data lens</p>
    <h3>Check what the data can prove.</h3>
    <p>
      The role needs someone who can interrogate data, separate signal from
      noise, and be honest about what is known versus what still needs testing.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Software lens</p>
    <h3>Turn analysis into something real.</h3>
    <p>
      The work does not stop at insight. The engineer side matters because the
      solution has to be maintainable, tested, and ready to ship.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Cloud lens</p>
    <h3>Move it to production.</h3>
    <p>
      Cloud skills let the work leave the notebook and land in a live system
      where people can actually use it.
    </p>
  </article>
</div>

## Why this role matters

- It reduces the gap between business intent and technical delivery.
- It keeps analysis connected to production impact.
- It combines stakeholder understanding with engineering discipline.
- It values shipping something useful over producing isolated work.

> My working definition: a forward deployment engineer is a business analyst,
> a data scientist, and a software engineer with cloud skills, all in one role
> with a production mindset.
