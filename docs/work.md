# Skill Stack

This page breaks the role into the core skills that support it. The goal is to
show how the business side, the data side, the software side, and the cloud
side fit together.

## Core skills

<div class="card-grid">
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Business analysis</p>
    <h3>Frame the problem.</h3>
    <p>
      Translate vague requests into concrete problems, decisions, constraints,
      and success metrics.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Data science</p>
    <h3>Test the signal.</h3>
    <p>
      Inspect the data, check assumptions, look for bias, and decide whether the
      evidence supports the proposed direction.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Software engineering</p>
    <h3>Build for maintainability.</h3>
    <p>
      Write code that can be reviewed, tested, maintained, and extended without
      turning into a one-off prototype.
    </p>
  </article>
  <article class="info-card">
    <p class="info-card__kicker">Cloud and delivery</p>
    <h3>Move the work into production.</h3>
    <p>
      Understand deployment paths, environments, monitoring, and the basics of
      operating a system once it is live.
    </p>
  </article>
</div>

## What strong FDE work looks like

- The business problem is clear.
- The data limitations are stated honestly.
- The implementation is production-ready.
- The delivery path is simple enough to support.

## Article template

Use this structure when you write a post:

1. The business problem
2. The data question
3. The technical approach
4. The production path
5. What changed after shipping
6. What you learned
